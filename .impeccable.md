# Impeccable Design Context — Didi

## Product
Didi ("eat" in Twi) — multi-tenant food ordering platform for Ghana.
Customers browse a marketplace of local kitchens, order, pay with Mobile
Money or card, and track delivery live. Restaurant owners get a dashboard
(menu, orders, payments, analytics) and a brandable storefront.

## Target audience
- **Customers**: Ghanaian, mobile-first, often mid/low-end Android on
  variable networks. Speed and tap-friendliness beat spectacle.
- **Owners**: small kitchen / chop bar operators, not tech experts.
  Dashboard must be obvious, forgiving, glanceable.
- **Riders**: quick in-and-out portal.

## Brand personality
Warm, appetizing, Ghana-proud, trustworthy, energetic but not loud.
Food photography is the hero; UI stays warm-neutral and lets tenant
brand colors shine on storefronts.

## Design system facts
- Canvas: `#FCFBFA` warm off-white (`--color-canvas`), hairline borders.
- Brand: orange ramp around `#FF6B35` (`brand-50…900`). Tenants override
  with their own `primary_color` on storefront surfaces.
- Display face: Bricolage Grotesque (`--font-display`) for headings,
  prices, brand moments. Body: Plus Jakarta Sans (`--font-sans`).
- Shadows: custom `--shadow-card` / `--shadow-card-hover` tokens only.
- Motion: 150–300ms ease-out, `.press` scale on tap, `.stagger-item`
  list entrances, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected globally.
- Icons: Lucide only, no emoji as structural icons. Food emoji allowed
  as *content* in cuisine chips.

## Hard rules
- Tenant storefronts must respect `tenant.primary_color` — never
  hardcode Didi orange on tenant-branded surfaces.
- Mobile app (apps/mobile) is a Capacitor static export that reuses web
  components via cross-alias — keep changes compatible with both.
- Min 44px touch targets, 16px input font on mobile (iOS zoom),
  safe-area padding on fixed bars.
